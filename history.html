BRB was started by Atty. Teofile Tanjuatco and authorized by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas to operate as a rural bank
The Securities Exchange Commission approved the extension of BRB's corporate life for another 50 years
Quezon Capital Rural Bank together with the families of Herbert Bautista and Renato Mercado acquired the majority stake in the Company
The Company increased its authorized capital from PHP 11.0mn to PHP 25.0mn
A group of Filipino and Australian investors led by Robert Guy Tesoriero, Gregory Stephen Adams, and Leah Felisa Faustino acquired the majority stake in the Company
BRB was granted a conditional Electronic Money Issuer (EMI) license by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)
The board approved to further increase the Company's authorized capital to PHP 100.0mn, but the Company is still waiting for BSP's approval at this August, before SEC filing.

The Company launched the first phase of the bank;s digital integration program and prepaid cash cards
AgriNurture Inc. Group bought into the Bank at 50% + 1

Applied for further P100.0m, bringing total authorized capital to P200M.
